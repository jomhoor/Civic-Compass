# Civic Compass — Axis Definitions

## Overview

The Civic Compass is an 8-dimensional vector $V = [v_1, v_2, ..., v_8]$.  
Each axis is a bipolar spectrum ranging from **-1.0 (Pole A)** to **+1.0 (Pole B)**.  
A score of 0.0 indicates no lean toward either pole.

---

## The 8 Axes

### 1. Economy
| Pole A (-1.0) | Pole B (+1.0) |
|:---|:---|
| **Regulated / Planned** | **Free Market / Laissez-faire** |

*What it measures:* Preference for state intervention in economic affairs vs. trust in market self-regulation.  
*Key tensions:* Wealth redistribution, taxation, public ownership, trade policy, minimum wage.

---

### 2. Governance
| Pole A (-1.0) | Pole B (+1.0) |
|:---|:---|
| **Authoritarian / Centralized** | **Libertarian / Decentralized** |

*What it measures:* Preferred concentration of political power. Centralized authority and strong leadership vs. distributed power and individual autonomy.  
*Key tensions:* Executive power, direct democracy, federalism, bureaucracy, term limits.

---

### 3. Civil Liberties
| Pole A (-1.0) | Pole B (+1.0) |
|:---|:---|
| **Security / Order** | **Liberty / Rights** |

*What it measures:* Willingness to trade individual freedoms for collective security and social order.  
*Key tensions:* Surveillance, free speech limits, policing, protest rights, censorship.

---

### 4. Society
| Pole A (-1.0) | Pole B (+1.0) |
|:---|:---|
| **Traditional / Conservative** | **Progressive / Reform** |

*What it measures:* Attitude toward social change, cultural norms, and moral frameworks.  
*Key tensions:* Family structure, gender roles, immigration integration, religious influence, identity politics.

---

### 5. Diplomacy
| Pole A (-1.0) | Pole B (+1.0) |
|:---|:---|
| **Nationalist / Sovereigntist** | **Internationalist / Globalist** |

*What it measures:* Preferred balance between national sovereignty and international cooperation.  
*Key tensions:* Foreign aid, international treaties, border policy, supranational institutions, military alliances.

---

### 6. Environment
| Pole A (-1.0) | Pole B (+1.0) |
|:---|:---|
| **Productivist / Growth** | **Ecologist / Sustainability** |

*What it measures:* Priority given to economic growth and industrial output vs. environmental protection and sustainability.  
*Key tensions:* Climate regulation, energy policy, urban development, animal rights, resource extraction.

---

### 7. Justice
| Pole A (-1.0) | Pole B (+1.0) |
|:---|:---|
| **Punitive / Retributive** | **Rehabilitative / Restorative** |

*What it measures:* Philosophy of justice. Punishment and deterrence vs. reform and reintegration.  
*Key tensions:* Prison policy, death penalty, drug decriminalization, juvenile justice, corporate accountability.

---

### 8. Technology
| Pole A (-1.0) | Pole B (+1.0) |
|:---|:---|
| **Regulation / Precaution** | **Acceleration / Innovation** |

*What it measures:* Stance on technological development pace and governance. Cautious regulation vs. permissionless innovation.  
*Key tensions:* AI regulation, data privacy, biotech ethics, automation, digital currency, platform governance.

---

## Axis Design Principles

1. **Bipolarity:** Every axis has two legitimate, defensible poles. Neither pole is inherently "correct."
2. **Independence:** Axes should be as orthogonal as possible. Your Economy score should not predict your Environment score. (This will be validated via factor analysis.)
3. **Universality:** Axes apply across cultures and political systems. Avoid framing that only makes sense in one country's context.
4. **Concreteness:** Each axis maps to real policy debates, not abstract philosophy.

## Score Interpretation

| Range | Label |
|:---|:---|
| -1.00 to -0.60 | **Strong** Pole A |
| -0.59 to -0.20 | **Lean** Pole A |
| -0.19 to +0.19 | **Centrist / Mixed** |
| +0.20 to +0.59 | **Lean** Pole B |
| +0.60 to +1.00 | **Strong** Pole B |
